<Section icon={Target} title="Core Gameplay and Discovery Mechanics">

The narrative centers on an island where a talking book named Mr. E suddenly falls from the sky. Mr. E contains information about unusual creatures but has lost the ability to "see" or remember the details written on his own pages. Yoshi steps in to help investigate these habitats, effectively acting as a field researcher.

The gameplay loop involves entering a chapter, finding a creature, and experimenting with it. You might carry a creature on your back, feed it, or jump on it to see how it reacts. These interactions are recorded as "discoveries" in Mr. E's pages.

| Interaction Type | Tool Used | Resulting Discovery |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Taste** | Tongue | Learn if a creature is sweet or bitter |
| **Inhale** | Breath/Suction | Some creatures spit out Smiley Flowers |
| **Carry** | Back/Hands | Use creatures to bloom flower buds |
| **Smash** | Ground Pound | Reveal creatures stuck in rocks or grass |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Discovery Tip">
Some discoveries are linked. If you find a creature is "sweet," try bringing it to a larger creature that looks hungry. This often triggers a "Big Discovery" that unlocks the next area.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="How to Complete a Chapter Discovery">

Progressing through the book requires more than just reaching the end of a level. You must satisfy Mr. E's curiosity by documenting specific creature behaviors. Each habitat contains a "Big Discovery" that serves as the primary goal for that chapter.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Enter the Habitat">
    Choose a chapter from Mr. E's menu. Each chapter represents a different environment, such as the lush Wildwoods or bubble-filled caves.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Identify Unusual Creatures">
    Look for creatures that stand out, such as the Crazee Dayzee or the bubble-blowing Glubbit. Observe their natural movement before interacting.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Experiment with Moves">
    Use Yoshi's moveset—Gobble, Jump, Throw Egg, or Carry. Watch for unique animations, such as a creature popping or scattering seeds into the air.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Record the Findings">
    Once a reaction is triggered, Mr. E will automatically record the discovery. Finding the "Big Discovery" will allow you to turn the page to the next chapter.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Chapter Name | Primary Environment | Featured Creatures |

| **Chapter 1: Wildwoods** | Lush Forest | Crazee Dayzee, Dandelions |
| **Chapter 2: Bubble Grotto** | Underwater/Caves | Glubbit, Slug-shaped Boomerangs |
| **Chapter 3: Skyward Peaks** | High Altitude | Croakaoke, Bunchabee |

Amiibo support is a significant part of the daily routine in Yoshi and the Mysterious Book. By tapping a compatible figure, Mr. E will read your fortune. Depending on the result, you receive in-game tokens that can be spent on hints for difficult discoveries.

| Amiibo Type | Daily Benefit | Token Yield |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Yoshi Series** | High Fortune Chance | 50 - 100 Tokens |
| **Mario Series** | Standard Fortune | 20 - 50 Tokens |
| **Other Series** | Random Reward | 10 - 30 Tokens |

<Callout type="info" title="Hardware Note">
This game will not run on the original Nintendo Switch or Switch Lite. It requires the enhanced processing power of the Switch 2 to render the book's dynamic page-turning effects.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Creature Interaction Guide">

To help you fill Mr. E's pages, you need to understand the basic behaviors of the creatures you encounter. Many creatures have dual purposes—they can be obstacles or helpful tools depending on how Yoshi treats them.

| Creature Name | Description | Discovery Hint |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Crazee Dayzee** | A flower with feet | Carry it to touch unbloomed buds |
| **Glubbit** | Blows large bubbles | Jump on it to blow a bubble for Yoshi |
| **Croakaoke** | Bird-like singer | Watch how it reacts to music notes |
| **Bunchabee** | Flying insect | Catch it with a tongue for a sweet taste |
| **Dandelion** | Floating plant | Touch to scatter and break nearby rocks |

Interaction isn't just about combat. In fact, many creatures are friendly. For example, finding a "Big Guy" who is snoring in the ground requires you to find a specific food item to wake him up. This "Appetite for Discovery" is the core theme of the game's puzzles.

<Callout type="tip" title="Creature Naming">
