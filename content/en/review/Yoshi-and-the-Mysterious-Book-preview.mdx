<Section icon={Search} title="Mastering the Discovery System">

In this **Yoshi and the Mysterious Book preview**, we found that the core gameplay loop revolves entirely around "Discoveries." Unlike previous entries where you merely defeated enemies, here you are a researcher. Every creature has a set of hidden characteristics that you must uncover to complete Mr. E’s pages. These discoveries range from simple physical reactions to complex inter-species interactions.

Discoveries are categorized into three main types, each rewarding you with stamps and dopamine-inducing visual feedback.

| Discovery Category | Description | Reward Type |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Biological** | How a creature reacts to being eaten or jumped on | Creature Data & Naming |
| **Environmental** | Breaking specific objects or reaching hidden areas | Level Progression |
| **Interaction** | How two different species behave when placed together | Rare Stamps & Tokens |

<Callout type="warning" title="Discovery Warning">
Revisiting levels is mandatory for 100% completion. Some discoveries only become available after you meet creatures in later chapters and bring their "knowledge" back to earlier pages.
</Callout>

The game provides a robust hint system. By spending **Tokens**—which can be earned through gameplay or by tapping Amiibo—Mr. E will provide clues about missing discoveries. This prevents players from "flailing in the dark" when trying to figure out hyper-specific interactions, such as what happens when a specific bird eats a spicy pepper.

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Chapter Progression & The Summoning Mechanic">

Progression in **Yoshi and the Mysterious Book preview** is divided into themed chapters. While early levels focus on teaching you about one specific creature, the game’s depth truly opens up in the later stages. Specifically, the end of Chapter 6 introduces a mechanic that recontextualizes the entire experience.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Research">
    Explore Chapters 1 through 5, learning the basics of creature interaction and collecting Smiley Flowers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Unlock Summoning">
    Reach the final level of Chapter 6 to unlock the ability to summon any previously met creature at will.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Complex Puzzle Solving">
    Use your summoned allies to scale waterfalls, dig through mountains, or fight specialized enemies.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Final Exam">
    Apply all biological knowledge to navigate the final "Mysterious" pages of Mr. E.
  </Step>
</Steps>

This summoning system is the "spark" of the game, transforming your research into a tactical toolkit. It allows for creative problem-solving that isn't present in the earlier, more guided sections of the game.

<Callout type="info" title="Progression Info">
It takes approximately 8 hours to reach the end of Chapter 6. Completionists looking to find every discovery and Smiley Flower can expect an additional 5-10 hours of gameplay.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Collectibles and the Bizarre UI Rewards">

Smiley Flowers remain the primary collectible in **Yoshi and the Mysterious Book preview**, usually hidden in classic platforming spots. However, the reward for collecting them is one of the most unconventional systems in Nintendo history. Instead of new levels or costumes, you unlock "Nonsensical HUD options."

These UI elements range from useful (a health bar or a radar) to purely aesthetic or confusing (water quality graphs and temperature gauges). While they don't impact the difficulty, they represent the game's commitment to its quirky, experimental identity.

| UI Element | Cost (Flowers) | Functionality |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Companion Log** | 5 | Displays chat history with Mr. E |
| **Flavor Graph** | 10 | Tells you if a creature tastes sweet or sour |
| **Flower Radar** | 15 | Points toward nearby hidden collectibles |
| **Health Gauge** | 20 | Shows Yoshi's remaining health (Visual only) |
| **Atmosphere Meter** | 25 | Measures in-game humidity and wind speed |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Strategy">
Prioritize unlocking the **Flower Radar** first. It significantly reduces the time spent replaying levels to find those last few elusive Smiley Flowers hidden behind background objects.
</Callout>
