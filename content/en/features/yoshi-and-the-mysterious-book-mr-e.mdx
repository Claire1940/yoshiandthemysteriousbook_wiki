### Mr. E's Names Across the Globe

The name "Mr. E" itself is a pun on "mystery" in English. Here's a look at how his name is translated and adapted in other languages, often retaining a similar playful or thematic element:

| Language | Name                   | Meaning / Note                                                                 |
| :------- | :--------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Japanese | フカシギ (*Fukashigi*) | From "不可思議" (*fukashigi*, "mysterious", "mystery")                            |
| Dutch    | Graaf Goedboeck        | Alliteration with *graaf* ("count") and a pun on *goed boek* ("good book")      |
| French   | Mysterius              | From *mystère* ("mystery")                                                     |
| German   | Enzo                   | Pun on *Enzyklopädie* ("encyclopedia")                                         |
| Italian  | Enzo / N. Ciclopedio   | Short for "professor Sapienzo" (wisdom) / From *enciclopedia* (encyclopedia)   |
| Korean   | 신기한 (*Singihan*)     | Pun on "신기하다" (*singihada*, "to be mysterious") and surname "한" (*Han*) |
| Portuguese | Professor N. Igma      | Pun on "enigma"                                                                |
| Spanish  | Profesor Leo           | Homophone on *leo* ("I read"), possibly acronym for *Libro de especies ocultas* |

This linguistic diversity highlights the thoughtful design behind **Mr. E's** character, making him resonate with players worldwide.
