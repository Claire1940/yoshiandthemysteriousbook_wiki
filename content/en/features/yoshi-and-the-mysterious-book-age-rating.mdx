<Callout type="tip" title="Understanding PEGI Ratings">
The PEGI rating system evaluates games based on content like violence, language, fear, and discrimination. A **PEGI 7** rating usually means the game contains scenes or sounds that might be frightening to very young children, or very mild forms of violence that are non-realistic or directed at fantasy characters.
</Callout>

### Key Rating Descriptors

The PEGI 7 rating for *Yoshi and the Mysterious Book* is specifically attributed to two content descriptors: **Fear** and **Violence**. These descriptors indicate the presence of certain elements that, while mild, warrant the age recommendation.

| Descriptor | Explanation                                                                                                | In-Game Example (Mild)                                                                                                              |
| :--------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Fear**   | Non-realistic, harmless scenes or sounds that might be frightening to younger children.                    | Encountering slightly spooky creatures or environments, unexpected (but not truly harmful) jumpscares.                             |
| **Violence** | Very mild, non-realistic forms of violence, often in a cartoonish or fantasy context, without detailed injury. | Yoshi consuming enemies, bouncing on them, or using eggs as projectiles; enemies disappearing without graphic depictions of harm. |
