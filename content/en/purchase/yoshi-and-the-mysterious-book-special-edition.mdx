<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Core Mechanics and Discovery Gameplay">

The gameplay loop in Yoshi and the Mysterious Book revolves around the "Discovery" system. Instead of simply reaching the end of a level, Yoshi must experiment with every object and creature he encounters. Every action you take—whether it is jumping on a creature, swallowing it, or carrying it to a different part of the map—reveals a new entry for Mr. E's encyclopedia. These discoveries provide "Stars," which act as the primary currency for unlocking subsequent chapters and biomes.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Enter a Habitat">
    Select a chapter from Mr. E's pages and jump into a hand-drawn biome like the Wildwoods.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Interact with Creatures">
    Use Yoshi's tongue to taste creatures or pick them up. Some creatures, like the flower-like Crazee Dayzee, can be carried on your back.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Experiment with Elements">
    Combine creatures with environmental objects. For example, touching dandelions with a specific creature might cause them to scatter and break obstacles.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Record Discoveries">
    Once an interaction is successful, Mr. E records the data. Collecting enough data points unlocks the "Big Discovery" for that area.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Action | Result | Discovery Type |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gobble** | Taste the creature and potentially turn it into an egg | Dietary Fact |
| **Carry** | Use the creature's unique ability (e.g., blowing bubbles) | Ability Fact |
| **Inhale** | Some creatures spit out Smiley Flowers when they inhale others | Interaction Fact |
| **Jump** | Check if the creature pops, squishes, or reacts aggressively | Physical Fact |

<Callout type="tip" title="Discovery Hint">
Some discoveries can only be made by bringing a creature from a later chapter back to an earlier habitat. Always revisit old pages with new friends!
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Biomes and Creature Habitats">

The world within Mr. E is divided into several distinct biomes, each offering unique challenges and creatures. Progressing through the game requires players to earn stars by completing encyclopedia entries. While the Wildwoods serve as the introductory area, later stages like the Seaside and Mountain Top introduce complex verticality and elemental puzzles.

**Key Creatures to Watch For:**
- **Crazee Dayzee**: Found in multiple colors across the Wildwoods; can bloom flower buds.
- **Glubbit**: A creature that blows large bubbles Yoshi can hop into to reach high platforms.
- **Croakaoke**: Bird-like creatures that sing; their music can trigger environmental changes.
- **Bunchabee**: Fast-moving insects that Yoshi can catch with his sticky tongue for high point rewards.

| Biome | Primary Creature | Unique Mechanic | Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wildwoods** | Crazee Dayzee | Flower blooming and rock breaking | Easy |
| **Seaside** | Glubbit | Bubble floating and water physics | Medium |
| **Mountain Top** | Croakaoke | Sound-based puzzles and wind currents | Hard |

<Callout type="info" title="Rival Appearance">
Keep an eye out for Bowser Jr. and Kamek. They occasionally appear in habitats to steal discoveries or sabotage Yoshi's progress.
</Callout>
